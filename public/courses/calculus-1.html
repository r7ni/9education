Calculus 1

Certified by Carleton University

This course introduces the fundamental concepts of differential and integral calculus.

Duration: Approx. 30 hours

Lectures

Lecture 1: Functions and Graphs Review

This lecture reviews key concepts in functions and graphs, including the vertical and horizontal line tests, domain and range, inverse functions, and composite functions. They also cover polynomial properties, trigonometric identities, and solving logarithmic equations. Additionally, hyperbolic functions are introduced.

Access Powerpoint →

Lecture 2: Derivatives, Definitions, and Basic Rules

This lecture covers fundamental calculus concepts, including an introduction to derivatives and integrals, the definition of limits, and strategies for evaluating limits graphically, numerically, and algebraically. They also discuss the limit laws, which are essential for solving limit problems.

Access Powerpoint →

Lecture 3: Applications of Derivatives

This lecture covers continuity and the precise definition of a limit. They explain different types of discontinuities, such as when a function is undefined at a point, when the limit does not exist, or when the limit exists but does not match the function's value. The precise definition of a limit is introduced, likely using the epsilon-delta approach.

Course Syllabus

Course Overview

This course provides a comprehensive introduction to differential and integral calculus, covering limits, derivatives, integrals, and their applications.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the concept of limits and continuity
  • Master differentiation techniques and their applications
  • Develop skills in integration and its applications
  • Apply calculus to solve real-world problems
